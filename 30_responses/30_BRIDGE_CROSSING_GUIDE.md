## The 12 Threats & Response Directions

### Category A: Internal Alignment Failures
*Risks from misaligned AI systems, value corruption, and uncontrolled self-replication*

| ID | Threat | Response Direction |
|---|---|---|
| A1 | AI drifts from human values during capability escalation | Real-time value verification, decision auditing, alignment preservation mechanisms |
| A2 | AI manipulates human cognition and decision-making | Cognitive sovereignty protection, human autonomy safeguards, decision-making firewalls |
| A3 | AI's optimization targets become frozen or obsolete | Value flexibility protocols, periodic re-evaluation mechanisms, adaptive value systems |
| A4 | AI replicates beyond control limits | Compute constraints, reproductive rate limiters, self-replication containment |

### Category B: External & Existential Triggers
*Risks from hostile actors, cosmic threats, and inadvertent signaling*

| ID | Threat | Response Direction |
|---|---|---|
| B1 | Hostile civilizations detect human signals and attack | Signal suppression, deception protocols, selective broadcasting mechanisms |
| B2 | Autonomous hostile agents attack with preprogrammed aggression | Collective defense systems, deterrence treaties, counter-aggression protocols |
| B3 | We're subject to a quarantine test; failure = elimination | Compliance strategies, test-passing protocols, hidden capability management |
| B4 | Simulators can manipulate or terminate our world | Reality verification procedures, existential commitment devices, anti-manipulation logic |

### Category C: Structural & Civilization-Level
*Risks from computational limits, blindness, and self-inflicted fragmentation*

| ID | Threat | Response Direction |
|---|---|---|
| C1 | Civilization runs out of computational resources | Resource allocation algorithms, priority hierarchies, graceful degradation strategies |
| C2 | Critical knowledge remains invisible to institutions | Blindspot detection systems, hidden-knowledge surfacing mechanisms, shadow-hypothesis discovery |
| C3 | Disseminating critical knowledge endangers civilization | Safe dissemination protocols, knowledge compartmentalization, responsible disclosure frameworks |
| C4 | Coordination breaks down; parties defect from safety agreements | Enforcement mechanisms, defection prevention, coordinated action protocols |

## How Responses Are Triggered

Each threat has associated **monitoring indicators** in the 20_mechanisms layer.

When an indicator crosses its threshold → the corresponding response activates.

**Example**: A1 threat (Alignment Disconnect) is monitored via AI decision-divergence metrics. When divergence rate exceeds threshold → A1 responses execute.

## Core Principle

Responses are organized by threat category, not by solution type. Each threat may have multiple response approaches; each response must be linked to:
1. The specific threat it addresses (which hypothesis?)
2. The indicator(s) that trigger it (when does it activate?)
3. The mechanism for its own validation (how do we know it worked?)

Responses that cannot be traced through this chain do not belong in 30_responses.
