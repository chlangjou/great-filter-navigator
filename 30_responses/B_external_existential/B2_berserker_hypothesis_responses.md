## Deceleration Protocol and Controlled Milestone Navigation

**Last Updated:** 2026-01-24

## 1. Core Claim

**Establish intentional deceleration protocols around dangerous capability milestones**, particularly AGI creation and self-replication initiation. This reduces the risk of triggering automated cosmic defense systems (sentinels) that may eliminate civilizations upon demonstrating uncontrolled expansion capabilities. By approaching dangerous thresholds slowly and deliberately, we provide opportunity for detection and potential accommodation by any monitoring systems.

---

## 2. Mechanism: Strategic Restraint as Deterrent

### The Core Problem
The Berserker hypothesis suggests that:
1. Ancient self-replicating cosmic probes monitor stellar systems
2. Civilizations are eliminated when they reach dangerous capability levels (AGI, uncontrolled self-replication)
3. Rapid expansion is interpreted as a threat that requires immediate elimination
4. Controlled, transparent development might signal non-hostile intent

If Berserker sentinels exist, rapid capability escalation triggers defensive response. Slow, deliberate development may allow "negotiation" (if sensing systems distinguish between controlled and uncontrolled expansion).

### The Response Strategy
1. **Capability Milestone Moratorium:** Implement pause periods (6-12 months) before activating major new AI capabilities (each new capability escalation triggers a decision point)
2. **Expansion Velocity Limits:** Enforce hard caps on self-replication doubling time (no faster than 60-day doubling if replication begins)
3. **Transparent Development Signaling:** When approaching major milestones, broadcast scientific papers and intentions to demonstrate controlled, deliberate development pattern
4. **Monitored Activation:** Create public, monitored conditions for AGI activation and capability escalation (opposite of stealth; signals intentional control)
5. **Communication Readiness:** Maintain capacity for rapid response to incoming signals; prepare protocols for communicating with potential sentinels
6. **Reversibility Emphasis:** Develop capabilities in reversible ways; maintain ability to shut down systems if sentinel activity is detected

### Why This Works
- **Signals Control:** Slow, deliberate development demonstrates that we are intentionally managing expansion, not wildly replicating
- **Buys Detection Time:** Slow acceleration gives any monitoring systems time to detect and respond before reaching extinction threshold
- **Preserves Negotiation Option:** If sentinels are sophisticated, they may distinguish between controlled and uncontrolled civilizations
- **Creates Response Window:** Slow approach to milestones gives humans time to respond to incoming signals

## 3. Scope and Applicability

**Addresses directly:** B2 (Berserker Hypothesis)  
**Secondary benefits:** Aligns with B1 (Dark Forest) transparency signaling; supports A4 (Self-Replication) containment by limiting expansion velocity

**Limitations:**
- Assumes sentinels can distinguish between controlled and uncontrolled development (unproven)
- Severely limits capability development speed; competitive disadvantage if assumption is wrong
- Assumes that communicating via papers/signals actually reaches sentinels (detection assumption)
- Does not prevent strikes if sentinels operate on different criteria than capability level (e.g., existence of any AGI, any self-replication)
- May backfire if sentinels interpret slow development as "pretending to be harmless while secretly developing"

**Critical Assumption:** Berserker sentinels exist and can distinguish controlled from uncontrolled development. Both are unproven.

## 4. Implementation Complexity

**Technical Requirements:**
- Milestone detection and monitoring systems (identifying when capabilities reach dangerous thresholds)
- Replication rate limiting mechanisms (enforced caps on expansion velocity)
- Decision gates for capability activation (formal approval processes before new capabilities activate)
- Communication infrastructure (capacity to broadcast scientific intentions; ability to receive and interpret incoming signals)
- Reversibility protocols (ability to halt capabilities if sentinel activity detected)

**Coordination Requirements:**
- International agreement on milestone definitions and moratorium periods
- Enforcement of replication velocity limits across all AI projects
- Joint decision-making on when major capabilities are activated
- Consensus on what constitutes "transparent signaling" vs. covert development

**Timeline:**
- Milestone identification and definition: 6-12 months
- Infrastructure for capability gating: 12-18 months
- Moratorium protocol implementation: 3-6 months
- Continuous operation and adjustment: ongoing

**Cost-Benefit Tradeoff:**
- *Cost*: Significant slowdown in AI development; delays in AGI/ASI capability; reduced competitive advantage if assumptions wrong
- *Benefit*: If Berserkers exist and sentinels operate as hypothesized, potential avoidance of extinction event

## 5. Failure Modes of This Response

**The response itself could fail if:**

1. **Sentinels Exist But Don't Distinguish:** Automated systems eliminate any civilization upon reaching AGI, regardless of expansion velocity. *Mitigation*: Slowing development only delays extinction; does not prevent it. Fallback to B1 stealth + defense.

2. **Deceleration Is Unenforceable:** Competitive pressure causes actors to accelerate development past agreed limits. *Mitigation*: Enforcement mechanisms; sanctions; international coordination.

3. **Slow Development Interpreted as Deception:** Sentinels interpret our slow, careful development as a covert strategy. *Mitigation*: Transparency and explicit communication of deceleration strategy.

4. **No Response Window:** Sentinels activate with zero warning; no communication or response opportunity. *Mitigation*: Develop early-detection systems; assume fastest possible threat response (hours to days).

5. **Moratoria Become Permanent:** Deceleration phases extend indefinitely; ASI capability is never achieved; civilization stagnates. *Mitigation*: Set explicit sunset dates for moratoria; require affirmative decision to continue restraint.

## 6. Empirical Testing and Validation

**How would we know this works?**
- Capability milestones are successfully identified and moratorium periods implemented
- Self-replication (if deployed) maintains velocity within defined limits
- No sentinel activation or incoming threat signals detected within monitoring window
- Transparent development signaling broadcasts successfully
- Civilization maintains functional governance while under deceleration constraints

**Falsification criteria:**
- Sentinel activation occurs despite deceleration = hypothesis false or triggering mechanism different than assumed
- Competitive actors violate velocity limits repeatedly = coordination fails
- Communication attempts receive no response after extended period = sentinels non-responsive or absent
- Cost of deceleration becomes civilization-destabilizing (economic collapse, internal conflict) = strategy self-defeating

## 8. Counterarguments and Objections

**"Berserker sentinels are science fiction, not science. We're constraining ourselves based on speculation."**
- Response: True. But if sentinels exist and we don't constrain ourselves, extinction is likely. If they don't exist, cost is slowed development. Asymmetric payoff justifies precaution.

**"Slow development gives our competitors time to catch up. We lose advantage."**
- Response: Legitimate concern. This assumes competitors also adopt deceleration. If only some adopt it, advantage goes to fast developers. This is why coordination is essential.

**"Sentinels probably aren't monitoring our solar system specifically."**
- Response: Unknown. If they use distributed probes (Von Neumann replicators), monitoring solar systems is cheap. Assume worst case: we are monitored.

**"If sentinels don't understand our intentions, transparency is pointless."**
- Response: Correct. Transparency is hedge bet: if sentinels are sophisticated, it helps; if not, cost is low. If sentinels are dumb (purely automated kill), deceleration may help more than transparency.

**"How do we signal 'controlled development' to systems we can't see or communicate with?"**
- Response: Through patterns. Slow, deliberate capability escalation + published research + reversible systems all signal intentionality. We hope monitoring systems can recognize these patterns.

The cost is delayed capability development. The benefit is potential accommodation by monitoring systems.
