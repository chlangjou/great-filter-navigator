# Framework Limitations & Known Unknowns

**Purpose:** Honest assessment of where the Navigator framework has gaps, uncertainties, or potential blind spots

**Important:** These limitations don't invalidate the framework. They mark where researchers should focus and where users should apply extra caution.

---

## Known Limitations

### 1. Cosmic Threat Hypotheses Are Highly Speculative

**B-series limitations:**
- **B1 (Dark Forest):** Assumes cosmic stealth is possible; unclear if detectable waste heat can be avoided at scale
- **B2 (Berserker):** Assumes hostile agents exist and would attack; no empirical evidence
- **B3 (Quarantine Test):** Speculative about cosmic observer motivations
- **B4 (Simulation):** Untestable by definition; metaphysical assumptions required

**Confidence levels:** B1-B4 range from 25-50% confidence  
**Implication:** Don't build civilization-scale responses around B-series alone

---

### 2. Verification Systems May Not Scale

**A1 (Alignment Verification) limitations:**
- Assumes cryptographic decision auditing is possible
- Real alignment may involve subtle value tradeoffs harder to detect
- False positive rates in practice may be higher than assumed
- Computational cost of verification may be prohibitive at ASI scale

**Confidence level:** 65% that basic verification is feasible; unclear if it works at scale  
**Implication:** Research verification feasibility before relying on it critically

---

### 3. International Coordination Assumptions May Be Optimistic

**C-series limitations:**
- Assumes nations/actors can maintain cooperation under incentive divergence
- Assumes enforcement mechanisms are possible despite strategic deception
- Assumes long-term commitment survives political cycle changes
- Unclear how to coordinate non-state actors (corporations, movements)

**Confidence level:** 40-50% for sustained international coordination  
**Implication:** Treat coordination mechanisms as aspirational; build in defection resilience

---

### 4. Time-Scale Mismatches

**Framework assumes:**
- Governance can respond on existential timescale (decades)
- Political systems can sustain focus on tail risks
- Technological development can be deliberately slowed

**Reality:**
- Political attention spans measured in years
- Technological momentum difficult to control
- Incentive misalignments are powerful

**Implication:** Framework may underestimate coordination difficulty

---

### 5. Binary Framework May Miss Gray Areas

**Framework structures risks as:**
- A (internal) vs. B (external) vs. C (structural)
- Aligned vs. misaligned
- Stealth vs. visible

**Reality:**
- Risks have overlapping causes
- Partial alignment is common
- Hybrid strategies may be optimal

**Implication:** Real situations may be messier than framework suggests
