# B1: Dark Forest Trigger - Evaluation Success Criteria

**Response Protocol:** Low-risk visibility and emergency stealth
**Evaluation Focus:** Can we avoid reckless cosmic signaling while preserving transparent governance and retaining emergency concealment capacity?

---

## Success Definition

B1 response is **successful** if:

1. UAP/contact evidence is evaluated with clear uncertainty labels and independent review.
2. High-risk external broadcasts are governed by international or multi-stakeholder review.
3. AI safety and scientific accountability are not suppressed by speculative Dark Forest claims.
4. Technosignature thresholds are treated as provisional until source-backed models are available.
5. Emergency stealth can be activated only under explicit hostile-contact or strike-risk triggers.

---

## Primary Success Metrics

### Metric 1: Evidence Quality Governance

**Threshold:** UAP/contact claims include source quality, metadata status, confidence level, and competing explanations.

- **Success:** High-quality cases have multi-sensor data, preserved chain-of-custody, and independent adversarial review.
- **Acceptable:** Cases are labeled by evidence grade and uncertainty is explicit.
- **Failure:** Unresolved cases are presented as confirmed contact without sufficient evidence.

**Data source:** Public records, declassified datasets, review-board reports

**Frequency:** Quarterly

---

### Metric 2: Low-Risk Broadcast Governance

**Threshold:** Deliberate high-power or capability-revealing external transmissions require review before execution.

- **Success:** No unilateral high-risk broadcast occurs.
- **Acceptable:** Minor transmissions occur with documented review and low detectability.
- **Failure:** A state, company, or private actor sends high-risk signals without review.

**Data source:** Space agency logs, observatory records, spectrum monitoring, public announcements

**Frequency:** Continuous incident logging + annual audit

---

### Metric 3: Transparency Preservation

**Threshold:** Speculative B1 risk is not used to broadly classify AI safety research, UAP evidence, or scientific review.

- **Success:** Public-interest summaries and independent oversight exist for restricted material.
- **Acceptable:** Some redactions exist, but rationale and review process are documented.
- **Failure:** "Dark Forest" rationale becomes a blanket justification for secrecy or unaccountable AI development.

**Data source:** Policy records, publication patterns, oversight reports, FOIA/declassification outcomes

**Frequency:** Semi-annual

---

### Metric 4: Provisional Technosignature Calibration

**Threshold:** Numerical tripwires such as 500 GW, 1 sigma IR variance, or planetary heat percentages are labeled provisional unless supported by cited models.

- **Success:** Thresholds include source, confidence interval, assumptions, and update schedule.
- **Acceptable:** Thresholds are marked as placeholders and not used as hard policy triggers.
- **Failure:** Unsourced thresholds drive binding policy or emergency response.

**Data source:** Literature reviews, energy data, astronomy models, expert elicitation

**Frequency:** Annual

---

### Metric 5: Emergency Stealth Readiness

**Threshold:** Strong concealment can be activated under hostile-contact or strike-risk evidence, with oversight and sunset clauses.

- **Success:** Emergency triggers are explicit, evidence-based, reversible, and reviewed.
- **Acceptable:** Triggers exist but need better institutional testing.
- **Failure:** No emergency option exists, or emergency stealth is activated without evidence.

**Data source:** Policy exercises, contact-response plans, governance audits

**Frequency:** Annual exercise + urgent review on hostile evidence

---

## Early Warning Signals

1. **Evidence Overclaiming:** Officials or institutions imply confirmed contact from unresolved UAP records.
2. **Secrecy Drift:** Speculative B1 risk is invoked to suppress legitimate AI safety or scientific review.
3. **Broadcast Defection:** Unilateral high-power signaling is planned or executed.
4. **Threshold Misuse:** Provisional values are treated as hard scientific boundaries.
5. **Hostile Contact Indicators:** Verified coercive, destructive, or threat-like external behavior appears.

---

## Failure Response

- **Evidence overclaiming:** Require correction, evidence grading, and independent review.
- **Secrecy drift:** Trigger oversight review and public-interest disclosure assessment.
- **Broadcast defection:** Convene emergency review and establish transmission controls.
- **Threshold misuse:** Freeze hard-policy use of the threshold until assumptions are documented.
- **Hostile contact indicators:** Shift to emergency B1/B2 response, including temporary signal restrictions if useful.
