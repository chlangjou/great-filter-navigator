# D1.2: Global Network Fragmentation Index

**Associated Hypothesis:** `10_hypotheses/D_infrastructure_resilience/D1_orbital_trap.md`  
**Core Question:** Is the global coordination substrate degrading into isolated, coercible regional networks?

---

## 1. Measurement Definition

The Global Network Fragmentation Index measures whether satellite loss, undersea cable disruption, national firewalls, port blockades, or platform sovereignty policies are breaking the global Internet and data commons into regional intranets.

Inputs:
- Undersea cable outage frequency and repair latency.
- Cross-border bandwidth concentration and chokepoint exposure.
- Satellite backhaul dependency for remote regions.
- State-mandated network isolation, routing controls, and Starlink-style service denial.
- Cross-jurisdiction replication of critical AI model weights, safety logs, and governance records.
- Ability of independent institutions to verify AI behavior across regions.

**Assessment:**
- **NOMINAL:** global routing remains redundant and open enough for plural oversight.
- **CAUTION:** major regions can be isolated for days to weeks by cable, satellite, or policy disruption.
- **CRITICAL:** global AI governance and civilizational coordination fracture into state-controlled intranets.

---

## 2. Observable Signals

### Signal D1.2a: Physical Chokepoint Stress
- Cable cuts, landing-station attacks, or repair delays.
- Port blockades affecting cable repair ships or critical hardware movement.
- Regional dependence on a small number of orbital or submarine links.

### Signal D1.2b: Information Sovereignty Fragmentation
- National firewalls blocking global verification systems.
- Restrictions on satellite Internet and independent communication networks.
- Forced localization of AI models, logs, and compute governance.

### Signal D1.2c: ASI Continuity Risk
- Critical AI safety records stored under single-jurisdiction control.
- Failure to replicate model audits and alignment evidence across trusted independent institutions.
- Emergency shutdown or seizure capacity concentrated in one state or corporate actor.

---

## 3. Tripwire Thresholds

| Status | Tripwire | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| NOMINAL | No single region can isolate critical safety coordination for more than 24-72 hours | Maintain redundancy |
| CAUTION | One or more major regions can be isolated for a week through plausible cable, satellite, or policy action | Harden cross-border continuity |
| CRITICAL | AI safety governance, public evidence, or model continuity becomes controlled by isolated sovereign networks | Activate anti-capture and restoration protocols |
