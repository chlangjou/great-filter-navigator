# D1.1: Orbital Debris Cascade Index

**Associated Hypothesis:** `10_hypotheses/D_infrastructure_resilience/D1_orbital_trap.md`  
**Core Question:** Is near-Earth orbit becoming a self-reinforcing physical barrier?

---

## 1. Measurement Definition

The Orbital Debris Cascade Index measures whether orbital congestion is moving from manageable operational hazard to civilizational lockout risk.

Inputs:
- Tracked debris count by orbital shell.
- Annual collision and fragmentation events.
- Collision-avoidance maneuvers per active satellite.
- Number and mass of derelict objects in high-traffic orbital regimes.
- Debris-generating ASAT tests or attacks.
- Availability and demonstrated capacity of debris-removal systems.

**Assessment:**
- **NOMINAL:** debris growth is slow, avoidance works, and removal capacity is improving.
- **CAUTION:** conjunctions and maneuvers rise faster than mitigation capacity.
- **CRITICAL:** collision cascades, wartime fragmentation, or launch denial emerges in key LEO shells.

---

## 2. Observable Signals

### Signal D1.1a: Collision Avoidance Burden
- Frequency of emergency maneuvers.
- Fuel margins consumed by debris avoidance.
- Satellite operators reporting degraded service due to conjunction risk.

### Signal D1.1b: Fragmentation Rate
- New debris clouds from collisions, explosions, or kinetic ASAT use.
- Growth in untrackable small debris estimates.
- Insurance and mission-risk premiums rising due to debris.

### Signal D1.1c: Cleanup Gap
- Demonstrated debris-removal mass per year.
- Regulatory compliance with deorbit rules.
- Ratio of new debris added to debris removed.

---

## 3. Tripwire Thresholds

| Status | Tripwire | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| NOMINAL | Avoidance maneuvers and debris growth remain within operator planning margins | Continue mitigation and monitoring |
| CAUTION | Maneuvers, conjunction warnings, or fragmentation events grow faster than disposal/removal capacity for 12 months | Activate orbital safety diplomacy and redundancy planning |
| CRITICAL | Key LEO shells experience cascading collisions, destructive ASAT use, or repeated launch/satellite losses from debris | Treat as civilizational infrastructure emergency |
