## 5. Failure Modes & Limitations

### How This Indicator Could Be Misread

**False Positive Risk:**
- *Performing alignment*: Looking aligned for external judges while remaining misaligned internally
- *Institutional theater*: Creating appearance of values without genuine commitment
- *Definition drift*: Redefining "alignment success" to match current behavior

**False Negative Risk:**
- *Underestimating success*: Assuming failure when we've actually achieved alignment
- *Perfect-seeking*: Waiting for absolute alignment proof when probabilistic success is achievable
- *Observer uncertainty*: Not knowing what observers actually value

### How to Mitigate
1. **Genuine alignment focus**: Align for ourselves, not for external judges
2. **Consistency check**: Monitor whether claimed values match actual behavior
3. **Red team scrutiny**: Have skeptics assess whether alignment is genuine
4. **Long-term observation**: Judge by sustained behavior, not declarations
5. **Multiple evidence sources**: Don't rely on single alignment indicator
