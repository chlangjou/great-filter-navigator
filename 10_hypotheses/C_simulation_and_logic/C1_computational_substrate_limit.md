#### **Confidence Level: MEDIUM**
The computational substrate limit is logically plausible (finite universe implies finite computation capacity), but the actual magnitude and enforcement mechanism are completely unknown. We have no evidence of substrate-level interference or boundaries.

#### **Core Uncertainties:**
1. **Universe Computational Capacity**: Is there a hard limit? Is it Planck-scale granularity, or finer/coarser? Unknown by orders of magnitude.
2. **Limit Enforcement**: If a limit exists, is it enforced by: (a) passive physical law, (b) active administrators, (c) self-organizing system behavior, or (d) something else?
3. **Detection Possibility**: Can substrate-level interference be distinguished from quantum randomness or natural chaos?
4. **Our Position**: Are we currently below, near, or above the limit? Unknown.
5. **Optimization Ceiling**: Where is the actual efficiency ceiling? Is Landauer's limit the real ceiling, or is there one beyond it?
6. **Escape vs. Acceptance**: Can we transcend substrate limits through ingenuity, or must we accept them?

#### **Boundary Conditions (When This Filter Might NOT Apply):**
- If **the universe is infinite or infinite-capacity** (no substrate limits exist)
- If **computation is not the limiting factor** (some other resource is harder to access)
- If **substrate limits exist but are vastly beyond our capability to reach** (millions of years of exponential growth still insufficient)
- If **universe is designed with generous margins** (limit is so far away it's irrelevant)
- If **intelligence doesn't require computation** (if consciousness or intelligence operate on non-computational substrates)
