# Category D: Infrastructure Resilience and Orbital Security

**"The Ladder We Stand On"**

Category D covers failure modes where civilization does not lose intelligence, values, or cosmic opportunity directly, but loses the physical and network infrastructure required to keep those capacities integrated.

These risks are especially dangerous because they can be triggered below the level of extinction. A civilization can remain technically alive while becoming blind, fragmented, earthbound, and easy to capture by local powers.

## D1: Orbital Trap and Technical Sequestration

- **Core risk:** Low Earth orbit becomes unusable through debris cascades, anti-satellite conflict, or unmanaged launch growth.
- **Infrastructure coupling:** Satellite loss cascades into degraded navigation, communications, weather monitoring, disaster response, asteroid warning, and solar-storm forecasting.
- **ASI relevance:** Advanced AI may still run in terrestrial data centers, but its long-term resilience, plural governance, and outward expansion options are damaged if global networks and orbital access collapse.
- **Civilizational failure mode:** High technical capability persists locally, but civilization is confined to Earth, fragmented into intranets, and vulnerable to authoritarian capture or strategic sabotage.

## Why This Is a Separate Category

D-risks are not purely internal alignment failures (A), external cosmic threats (B), or abstract structural limits (C). They are **infrastructure chokepoints**: physical layers that make the rest of the Navigator possible.

## Risk Pattern Table

| Risk pattern | Trigger mechanism | Consequence for ASI / civilization |
|---|---|---|
| **Physical-layer severance** | Deliberate undersea cable cutting, port blockade, ground-station attack, power-grid disruption | AI governance and safety evidence split into regional data islands; global alignment feedback weakens; emergency coordination slows. |
| **Orbital barrier** | Kessler syndrome, debris-generating ASAT use, unmanaged orbital congestion | Civilization loses reliable satellite replacement and outward expansion capacity; it enters a high-technology but earthbound decline mode. |
| **Information-sovereignty fragmentation** | National firewalls, satellite Internet denial, forced data localization, sovereign intranets | ASI can become a single regime's digital leviathan rather than a plural, auditable partner for civilization. |

Category D should be monitored together with:
- **A2 Cognitive Resilience:** Loss of distributed knowledge systems accelerates agency erosion.
- **A4 Replication and Containment:** Destructive autonomous systems can target infrastructure layers.
- **B1/B2 External Signaling and Defense:** Loss of orbital awareness worsens cosmic stealth and defense.
- **C4 Outward Expansion:** Orbital denial blocks the first step toward sustained physical expansion.
