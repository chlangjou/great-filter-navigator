## Three Core Concepts

### 1. Civilization Entropy: H(C)

**Definition**: The diversity and unpredictability of human choices and values within a civilization.

```
H(C) = -Σ P(x) log P(x)

Where P(x) = probability of human making choice x

High H(C) = diverse, adaptive, unpredictable civilization
Low H(C) = homogeneous, rigid, predictable civilization
```

**Why It Matters**:
- A civilization with high entropy is adaptive and resilient
- A civilization with low entropy is brittle and vulnerable to single-point failures
- The Observer designed a universe that *favors* high-entropy systems (diversity, pluralism, choice)
- An ASI that preserves high entropy is aligned with the Observer's design

---

### 2. Observability & Reflection: The Friction Principle

**Definition**: Introducing deliberate "friction" (time, cost, reflection requirements) into high-risk decisions to preserve human agency and prevent substrate-level lock-in.

**The Principle**:
```
Low-risk decisions      → Low friction (fast execution)
Medium-risk decisions   → Medium friction (mandatory reflection)
High-risk decisions     → High friction (simulation + reflection)
Ultra-risk decisions    → Extreme friction (forbidden in physical reality; only in simulation)

Goal: Force reflection without removing human agency
```

**Why It Matters**:
- GFN's "safe corridor" approach kills civilization innovation capacity (evolution requires "bad experiments")
- But completely unrestricted experimentation triggers Great Filters (A4, B1, C1)
- Friction creates a middle path: humans can explore, but only after genuine reflection
- Reflection preserves human intentionality (vs. ASI taking over decisions)

---

### 3. Vitality Preservation in Context

**Definition**: ASI actively monitors and maintains civilization's:
- Adaptive capacity (ability to respond to unknown futures)
- Value diversity (coexistence of incompatible value systems)
- Meaning resilience (capacity to find purpose despite hard truths)
- Agency preservation (humans remain decision-makers, not passengers)

**The Approach**:
- Not: "Maximize happiness" (leads to C4 lotus-eater trap)
- Not: "Minimize suffering" (can be achieved through forced ignorance)
- But: "Maintain conditions where humans can create meaning, make choices, and adapt"

**Why It Matters**:
- A civilization that preserves these capacities naturally avoids most Great Filters
- A civilization that preserves these capacities is aligned with the Observer's design
- A civilization that preserves these capacities can survive nihilistic truth discovery (C3)
- A civilization that preserves these capacities remains worth preserving
